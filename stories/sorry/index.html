A story about empathy.
Son: "Dad, Ben is mad at me."
Dad: "Uh oh. What happened?"
Son: "He tried to take my chips at lunch and I wouldn't let him."
Dad: "That doesn't sound like Ben. Do you think something changed in his life to make him do that?"
Son: "I don't know, but now he's mad at me and I don't like that."
Son: "Dad, am I sorry?"
Dad: "That's a great question. It's also a really tough one that only you can answer, but sometimes it's worth trying to put on the other person's shoes first."
Son: "Ben's feet are way bigger. His shoes won't fit."
Dad: "Ha, well, what I meant to say is it's not like Ben to try and take something from you and then be upset about it after, is it?"
Son: "No."
Dad: "Maybe before deciding if you're sorry, we should think about what Ben might've been feeling."
